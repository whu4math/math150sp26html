4.5 Exponential and Logarithmic Equations

Recall: Exponential and logarithmic functions are inverses of each other.

Exponential and Log Equations

  1. Solve exponential equations.

    1. \(5^{x-1}=125\)

      \[\begin{align*} 5^{x-1} &= 5^3 \\ x - 1 &= 3 \\ x &= 4 \end{align*}\]

    2. \(2^{1-x}=3\)

      \[\begin{align*} \ln 2^{1-x} &= \ln 3 \quad \text{(take $ln$ or $log$ on both sides)}\\ (1-x) \ln 2 &= \ln 3 \\ (1-x) &= \frac{\ln 3}{\ln 2} \\ x &= 1 - \frac{\ln 3}{\ln 2} \end{align*}\]

    3. \(3e^x=10\)

      \[\begin{align*} e^x &= \frac{10}{3} \\ \ln e^x &= \ln \frac{10}{3} \\ x &= \ln\left(\frac{10}{3}\right) \end{align*}\]

    4. \(8+e^{1-4x}=20\)

      \[\begin{align*} e^{1-4x} &= 12 \\ \ln e^{1-4x} &= \ln 12 \\ 1-4x &= \ln 12 \\ -4x &= \ln 12 - 1 \\ 4x &= 1 - \ln 12 \\ x &= \frac{1 - \ln 12}{4} \end{align*}\]

    5. \(5^x=4^{x+1}\)

      \[\begin{align*} \ln 5^x &= \ln 4^{x+1} \\ x \ln 5 &= (x+1)\ln 4 \\ x \ln 5 &= x\ln 4 + \ln 4 \\ x(\ln 5 - \ln 4) &= \ln 4 \\ x &= \frac{\ln 4}{\ln 5 - \ln 4} = \frac{\ln 4}{\ln(5/4)} \end{align*}\]

  2. Solve logarithmic equations. MUST CHECK THE SOLUTIONS!

    1. \(\log(3x+5)=2\)

      \[\begin{align*} 10^2 &= 3x + 5 \\ 100 &= 3x + 5 \\ 3x &= 95 \\ x &= \frac{95}{3} \end{align*}\]

      Check: \(\log\left(3\cdot\frac{95}{3} + 5\right) = \log 100 = 2\)

      \(x = \frac{95}{3}\) is a solution.

    2. \(\log_2 x+\log_2(x-3)=2\)

      \[\begin{align*} \log_2[x(x-3)] &= 2 \quad \text{combine two logs}\\ x(x-3) &= 2^2 \quad \text{exponential form}\\ x^2 - 3x &= 4 \\ x^2 - 3x - 4 &= 0 \\ (x-4)(x+1) &= 0 \\ x &= -1, 4 \end{align*}\]

      \(x = -1\): \(\log_2(-1)\) is undefined

      \(x = 4\): \(\log_2 4 + \log_2 1 = 2 + 0 = 2\)

      \(x = 4\) is a solution.

    3. \(\log_3(x+15)-\log_3(x-1)=2\)

      \[\begin{align*} \log_3 \frac{x+15}{x-1} &= 2 \\ \frac{x+15}{x-1} &= 3^2 = 9 \\ x + 15 &= 9(x-1) \\ x + 15 &= 9x - 9 \\ 24 &= 8x \\ x &= 3 \end{align*}\]

      Check: \(\log_3 18 - \log_3 2 = \log_3 9 = 2\)

      \(x = 3\) is a solution.

    4. \(\log_2x-\log_2(3x+1)=1\)

      \[\begin{align*} \log_2 \frac{x}{3x+1} &= 1 \\ \frac{x}{3x+1} &= 2 \\ x &= 2(3x+1) \\ x &= 6x + 2 \\ -5x &= 2 \\ x &= -\frac{2}{5} \end{align*}\]

      Check: \(\log_2\left(-\frac{2}{5}\right)\) is undefined.

      The equation has no solution.

Compound Interest Problems

Interest compounded \(n\) times per year: \(A(t) = P\left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}\)

Interest compounded continuously: \(A(t) = Pe^{rt}\)

  1. A man invests $5000 in an account that pays 8.5% interest per year, compounded quarterly.

    (a) Find the amount after 3 years.

    \[r = 8.5\% = 0.085, \quad n = 4\]

    \[A(t) = 5000\left(1 + \frac{0.085}{4}\right)^{4t}\]

    \[A(3) = 5000\left(1 + \frac{0.085}{4}\right)^{12} \approx \$ 6435.09\]

    (b) How long will it take for the investment to double?

    \[\begin{align*} 10000 &= 5000\left(1 + \frac{0.085}{4}\right)^{4t} \\ 2 &= \left(1 + \frac{0.085}{4}\right)^{4t} \\ \ln 2 &= \ln\left(1 + \frac{0.085}{4}\right)^{4t} \\ \ln 2 &= 4t \ln\left(1 + \frac{0.085}{4}\right) \\ t &= \frac{\ln 2}{4\ln\left(1 + \frac{0.085}{4}\right)} \approx 8.24 \text{ years} \end{align*}\]

  2. Find the time required for an investment of $5000 to grow to $8000 at an interest rate of 7.5% per year, compounded monthly.

    \[\begin{align*} 8000 &= 5000\left(1 + \frac{0.075}{12}\right)^{12t} \\ \frac{8}{5} &= \left(1 + \frac{0.075}{12}\right)^{12t} \\ \ln \frac{8}{5} &= \ln\left(1 + \frac{0.075}{12}\right)^{12t} \\ \ln \frac{8}{5} &= 12t \ln\left(1 + \frac{0.075}{12}\right) \\ t &= \frac{\ln \frac{8}{5}}{12\ln\left(1 + \frac{0.075}{12}\right)} \approx 6.29 \text{ years} \end{align*}\]
